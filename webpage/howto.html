How to Play Mancala

The Setup

The Mancala board has two large pits at the ends of the boards called the Mancala. Each player owns the one to his right. Between the two Mancalas are two rows of six pits. At the beginning of each game, each pit will have four stones for a total of 48 stones.

The Rules

  • Each player takes turns to move all stones from a pit and sow them counter-clockwise. The player's Mancala is included as a pit, but the opponent's is not.
  • If the last stone that is sown by the player lands in the Mancala, the player is allowed to move again.
  • If the last stone that is sown by the player lands in an empty pit and the opponent's pit across from that pit has stones, the player captures all the stones into his Mancala.
  • The game ends when one player's side of the board is completely empty of stones. The winner is the player with more stones.
